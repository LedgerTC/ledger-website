Market Overview

Investing in West Virginia

West Virginia offers among the nation's lowest acquisition costs ($130,000–$180,000 median) combined with minimal property taxes (0.58%). Charleston and surrounding areas drive state activity with steady rental demand from manufacturing and government employment. The state's business-friendly environment and landlord-friendly laws support portfolio operations.

Population growth is modest, reflecting regional economic challenges. For investors prioritizing cash flow and operational simplicity, West Virginia delivers compelling cap rates (8%+) and low carrying costs. The market suits buy-and-hold portfolios with long-term hold horizons.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are West Virginia's property tax rates?
West Virginia property tax averages 0.58% of assessed value, among the lowest in the nation. This creates exceptional DSCR cash-flow returns (8%+). No income tax on retirement income.
Are there landlord-friendly eviction laws?
West Virginia is landlord-friendly. Evictions require 3 days' notice. Court proceedings typically conclude within 2 weeks. No just-cause requirement. Deposits and entry fees are permissive.
Can landlords raise rents without restriction?
Yes. West Virginia has no rent control. Landlords can set and raise rents freely, enabling predictable DSCR underwriting.
What rental income can I expect in West Virginia?
Charleston rentals range $550–$750/month on $80K–$150K properties. Multi-units support $450–$650/unit. Cap rates of 8–10% are common. Exceptional cash-flow market.
