Who Ledger is, and why this page exists

Ledger Trade & Capital is a direct private lender for licensed builders and experienced real estate developers. We finance ground-up construction, DSCR rental, spec home, fix-and-flip, and portfolio term loans up to $5M, in 40 states plus Washington, D.C. We are not a broker and we are not a marketplace. Every loan on our book is underwritten, closed, and serviced by our team.

Kiavi (formerly LendingHome) is one of the largest volume lenders in the private-credit space, with an automated application flow, a jumbo program up to $10M, and coverage in 49 states plus D.C. They're a real peer on product breadth. The gap between us is in leverage, pricing, and workflow, not in product overlap.

Where each lender fits better

We're not going to tell you Ledger is better on every axis. Here's the honest read, based on what each of us publishes.

Where Ledger fits better

  • Higher published construction LTC. 90% of total project cost vs. Kiavi's 85%. On a $2M build, that's $100K less equity you have to put in at close.
  • Higher construction LTARV. 75% vs. Kiavi's 70%. Matters on deals where the exit value is the constraint, not the cost basis.
  • Lower published construction rate. 8.875% vs. Kiavi's “as low as 9.00%.” On $1M for 12 months, roughly $1,250 less interest.
  • First-time builder program. Ledger has a dedicated product for builders on their first ground-up; Kiavi doesn't publish a distinct first-time path.
  • Named underwriter, not a queue. You get one person on your deal from pre-approval through close. Kiavi's pitch is automated speed; ours is builder-workflow fit.
  • No prepay on construction, from day one. Ledger publishes no prepay penalty on GUC. Kiavi's rental product removes prepay after year 3; their construction product page doesn't specify.
  • Spec home and portfolio term loans under one lender. If your exit on the GUC is a sale plus a rental hold, Ledger can finance both. Kiavi does ground-up and rental separately; they don't publish a spec-home or portfolio-term product.

Where Kiavi fits better

  • Larger jumbo ceiling. $10M per loan vs. Ledger's $5M. On a single project over $5M, Kiavi is one of few private shops that will write it.
  • Broader state footprint. 49 states + DC vs. Ledger's 40 + DC. If your project is in a state we don't currently cover, that's decisive.
  • 100% rehab financing on ground-up. Kiavi publishes up to 100% of rehab cost on the New Construction / Infill product; Ledger rolls rehab into the 90% LTC figure rather than breaking it out.
  • Longer stated construction term. Up to 24 months plus extension options on their product page vs. Ledger's 12–18 months.
  • Published DSCR rate floor. Kiavi advertises “as low as 6%” on rental. Ledger's DSCR pricing is disclosed on a per-deal basis.
  • Fast automated application. If your deal is standard and you're comfortable with a tech-forward flow, Kiavi moves quickly. Worth knowing up-front that the automation is the product.
  • Higher published F&F LTARV. Up to 80% of ARV vs. Ledger's 75%. On pure flip deals with strong ARV comps, that's five points of additional leverage.
