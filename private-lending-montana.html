Market Overview

Investing in Montana

Montana's real estate market combines outdoor lifestyle appeal with relatively modest growth. Billings and Missoula drive state activity, with $365,000 and $330,000 medians respectively reflecting mountain region premiums. Property taxes are low (0.84%), and no state income tax on Social Security and retirement income attract retirees and remote workers.

Population migration is accelerating but from a small base. The state suits investors seeking lifestyle properties, vacation rentals, or land development with patient capital. DSCR lending opportunities exist but require careful underwriting of seasonal and population dynamics.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are Montana's property tax rates?
Montana property tax averages 0.84% of assessed value, among the lowest in the nation. No income tax on Social Security and retirement income. No capital gains tax on primary residences.
Are there landlord-friendly eviction laws?
Montana is landlord-friendly. Evictions require 3 days' notice for non-payment. Court proceedings typically conclude within 2–3 weeks. No just-cause requirement. Deposits and entry fees are permissive.
Can landlords raise rents without restriction?
Yes. Montana has no rent control. Landlords can set and raise rents freely, supporting predictable DSCR underwriting.
What are the seasonal and population dynamics in Montana?
Montana is experiencing 1.2% annual population growth, above historical averages. Tourism is seasonal in mountain areas. Billings offers more stable year-round rental demand than resort communities.
