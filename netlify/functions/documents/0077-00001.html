Credit Box

Ground-up construction credit parameters and eligibility guidelines. Effective March 2026. All transactions subject to underwriter review and IC approval.

Program Tiers
Standard GUC
1–9 residential units  ·  Loan < $5M  ·  Project costs < $5M
Large Development Project
10+ units, any MF/mixed-use, OR loan > $7M, OR project costs > $10M (all phases)
Sizing & Loan Metrics
Max LTC Max LTARV Max LTAIV Min Equity Max Loan
Standard GUC 90% / 85%¹ 75% 70%² 10% of TPC $5,000,000
Large Development 80% 70% 70% 10% of TPC Case-by-case
¹ 90% LTC requires: TLA ≤$3M, LTARV <70%, 3+ mo. interest reserve, 5+ GUC exits or $10M+ volume, 1–4 units only.
² Max 60% LTAIV if not yet permitted.
Loan Term 9–18 months · Interest only · Fixed rate · Extensions negotiable
Min Loan $150,000
Cash-Out Not permitted on GUC loans
Contingency ≥10% of total hard + soft costs required in budget
GC Fee ≥5% of construction costs in budget (if borrower not acting as GC), or provide GC contract
Day 1 Draws Only for work completed after appraisal date; requires IC approval
Eligibility & Geographic Restrictions
Eligible
  • Single family detached
  • Townhome
  • 2–4 unit multifamily
  • Full or partial subdivisions
  • Infill projects
Property Conditions
  • ≤5 acres
  • Loan >$3M: secondary CDA required (≤10% variance)
  • 5+ units: full Commercial Appraisal required
  • Listed 120+ days: +3rd-party valuation, ≥5% leverage reduction
  • Declining market: 10% reduction to LTC/LTARV
Ineligible Property Types
Condominium  ·  Ground lease  ·  Land trust  ·  Properties in litigation, rent-controlled, or deed restricted
Ineligible Markets
Western FL  ·  NYC 5 Boroughs  ·  Chicago (city)
MSA Leverage Adjustments
  • 5% LTC reduction: Bay Area CA · Austin · Boise · Baltimore · Chicago suburbs · Detroit · Sacramento · San Jose
  • Elevated risk (case-by-case): Jersey City · Philadelphia · NYC suburbs · Texas
  • Florida: 5% reduction in LTC/LTARV/LTAIV on no-rehab bridge

Deal Process Overview

Ledger manages the full lifecycle of each ground-up construction loan, from initial submission through final payoff. The five-step process below outlines each important milestone, the documentation required at each step, and how Ledger coordinates diligence, closing, and ongoing draw activity on behalf of Lord Abbett.

01
Step 01 Purchase

All new acquisitions are purchased in cash and aggregated for submission to the normalized closing process. Loan closing will occur no later than 6 months post acquisition to benefit from Delayed Purchase designation. Ledger will provide a guideline for eligible properties and necessary pre-approval to ensure all acquisitions qualify for the facility.

02
Step 02 Submission

Submissions are accepted on a rolling basis by providing a cloud-based folder. Third party reports will be returned to the shared folder.

Required Documents
  • Settlement Statement
  • Purchase & Sale Agreement
  • Line Item Budget
  • Floor Plan
  • Site Plan
  • SPV Operating Agreement
  • SPV EIN Letter
  • SPV Articles of Formation
03
Step 03 Diligence

Ledger coordinates and orders all required third-party reports through its Loan Origination System to streamline diligence and expedite closing. Lord Abbett will have visibility into due diligence progress through Ledger's borrower-facing LOS portal.

Third-Party Reports & Docs
  • Appraisal
  • Feasibility Report
  • Flood Certificate
  • Title
  • GC Contract
  • CDA Secondary Valuation (if applicable)
  • Commercial Appraisal Report (if applicable)
  • Builder Collateral Assignment Contract
  • Builder's Risk & General Liability Insurance
04
Step 04 Funding

Ledger coordinates each property closing individually and will schedule the required wet-signed documentation for execution at each closing.

Closing Documents
  • Loan Documents
  • Environmental Indemnification
  • Limited Guaranty
05
Step 05 Asset Management

Ledger manages each loan through its Asset Management System, where a line-item budget tracks disbursements and remaining escrow balances. Draw requests are submitted through the system and processed following an on-site inspection.

Draw Requirements
  • Lien Waivers
  • Invoices on Soft Costs
Ongoing Compliance
  • Entity Compliance
  • Liquidity Compliance
04

Underwriting Diligence

The following third-party reports are ordered and reviewed by Ledger during the underwriting phase to validate collateral, market conditions, and project feasibility.

Report Purpose & Requirements
Appraisal
Third-Party AMC		 Independent AMC appraisal ordered to support underwriting and validate the purchase price and projected ARV. Provides an objective collateral assessment and satisfies lender credit requirements.
Collateral Desktop Analysis (CDA)
Secondary Eval		 A CDA valuation is an independent property value estimate produced using automated valuation models (AVMs) and market data rather than a full appraisal. This secondary valuation is required in addition to a standard appraisal for transactions with >$3mm total loan amount.
Commercial Appraisal Report
Third-Party Eval		 A commercial appraisal report is a more comprehensive valuation than a standard residential appraisal, prepared using detailed income, sales comparison, and cost analyses to determine market value. For transactions with 5+ units on a single loan, Ledger requires a commercial appraisal in place of a standard appraisal.
Feasibility Report
Third-Party Eval		 Third-party evaluation of market conditions, construction costs, and projected financial performance. Used to determine whether the project is financially and operationally viable relative to LTC and appraised ARV.
Flood Certificate
FEMA Assessment		 Determines whether the subject property falls within a FEMA-designated flood zone. Allows Ledger to assess flood risk and determine whether flood insurance coverage is required.
Title Commitment
ALTA Endorsements		 Preliminary title report confirming legal ownership and identifying any liens, encumbrances, easements, or restrictions.

Required ALTA endorsements: ALTA 9, ALTA 14, ALTA 22, ALTA 27.
Additional (loan-structure dependent): ALTA 6 (ARM), ALTA 10 (Assignment), ALTA 32–33 (Construction Draw).
Mechanics lien coverage required — may be satisfied through endorsements or comparable structure.

Closing Deliverables

These documents must be executed and in final form prior to or at closing. Each must identify the borrowing entity and subject property address.

5.1
Settlement Statement
Itemizes all funds exchanged between buyer, seller, lenders, and third-party vendors at closing. Ledger reviews to confirm sources and uses of funds and verify transaction closed per agreed terms.
5.2
General Contractor Contract & License
Executed agreement between the borrower and GC defining scope, project cost, timeline, and responsibilities. Ledger reviews to verify construction pricing, delivery expectations, and risk allocation.
  • Must identify the GC entity, borrowing entity, and property address
  • Must be fully executed
  • A copy of GC's License is required for newly onboarded builders.
5.3
Builder's Risk & General Liability Insurance
  • Builder's Risk: covers property damage during construction; coverage = replacement cost or full loan amount
  • General Liability: protects against third-party injury or property damage claims
  • Insured must be the borrowing entity
  • Policy must include a lender mortgagee clause
  • GL coverage: $1MM per occurrence / $2MM aggregate
5.4
Builder Collateral Assignment Contract
Assigns the borrower's rights under the construction contract to Ledger as additional collateral. Allows Ledger to step into the contract upon borrower default.
  • Requires a fully executed GC contract prior to execution

Loan Servicing

Key processes and requirements for managing your loan after closing through the FCI servicing portal. Ledger will remain as the main point of contact and will be providing everything needed from something as complex as customized reporting to as simple as a loan pay-off.

5.1
Servicer Sign-Up / Add-On
Register your new loan with the servicing portal by providing:
  • Borrowing entity name
  • Property address, city, and zip code
  • Borrowing entity EIN
5.2
ACH Setup
Complete the ACH form in its entirety with:
  • Entity documentation
  • Bank account and routing number
  • Authorized signatory and user contact
5.3
Loan Payments
Payments are due on the 1st of each month, calculated as a function of the current unpaid principal balance. Access your interest payable and outstanding loan balance in the My Portfolio section of the FCI portal.
Due 1st of Month
5.4
Draw Portal Access
Within two business days after closing, you will be notified that a new loan has been added to your borrower portal. Your loan profile will display key loan attributes and allow you to begin submitting draws.
Within 2 Business Days
5.5
Payoffs
Loan payoffs can be requested directly through Ledger or via the Loan Payoff section of the FCI portal. Payoff statements include servicer fees and are provided within two business days of request.
Statements Within 2 Business Days
08

Draw Submissions

Draw submissions are managed through the Ledger asset management policy. All documentation must be submitted prior to draw disbursement.

Step / Item Description & Requirements
6.1    Initiate Draw Request Click Add Service and select Draw in the portal. This will open a draft draw submission for review.
6.2    Itemized Draw Submission Enter requested draw amounts at a line-item level. Each line item may be submitted as a dollar value or as a percentage of the budgeted amount.
6.3    Scheduling Select a primary and secondary contact, plus an earliest available inspection date. Use the additional notes field for lockbox codes, entrance instructions, or other access details. An inspector will reach out within 24 hours to confirm scheduling.
6.4    Lien Waiver A signed lien waiver is required with each draw as attestation that disbursed funds will be allocated for their intended use.
6.5    Budget Review The borrower should review the schedule of values for accuracy and completeness. The total budget will match the budget submitted and utilized at closing.
6.6    Project Completion Update Provide an estimated project completion date so Ledger can effectively asset-manage each project.
6.7    Permits Building and zoning permits must be in good standing to qualify for draw disbursements. If permits were not provided at closing, they must be uploaded with the first draw submission.
6.8    Wire Affidavit The borrower must provide and reconfirm wire instructions for receipt of funds. All new bank accounts will be verified via phone call to the primary account contact.
6.9    Title Date Down Required for all unit-based draws exceeding $250,000. An updated title search identifies any delinquent payments or liens from closing to the current date. All liens must be satisfied before disbursement.
6.10   Insurance All active loans must maintain appropriate insurance coverage. Ground-up construction loans require an active builder's risk policy. Upon project completion, coverage must convert to standard property/dwelling insurance.
09

Cost Allocation

Guidelines for how project costs are categorized, validated, and credited toward draw disbursements.

7.1
Soft Costs
Service-related budget line items not validated by on-site inspection.
Examples
Architectural reports, engineering, permits, surveys, project planning
Required Documentation
  • Invoice documentation
  • Proof of payment (receipt, payment confirmation, or bank statement)
7.2
Hard Costs
All budget line items credited through on-site physical inspection.
Examples
Demolition, plumbing, HVAC, electrical, framing, insulation, painting, masonry, millwork, concrete, roofing, appliances, flooring
7.3
Deposits / Materials
Important
Credit is only allocated to completed work. Ledger does not advance funding for pre-work deposits or materials that have not yet been installed.
7.4
Percentage Completion
Certain ancillary costs required during construction (including GC fees and contingencies) are credited by percentage of completion rather than completed work.
7.5
Disbursement Credit
Once the inspection is completed and all documentation is submitted, an allocation of credit is approved in the Draw portal. Any reduction in credit will include an explanation. Borrowers may challenge by submitting photos, city inspection reports, or contacting the inspector directly.
7.6
Disbursement Amounts
The disbursement amount is a percentage of total allocated credit per the schedule of values, in accordance with the overall advance rate.
Example Rate
Advance RateDisbursement
85%$85 per $100 credit
