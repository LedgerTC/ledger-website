Market Overview

Investing in Florida

Florida remains America's premier real estate investment destination, driven by no state income tax, sustained population migration, and diverse regional markets. Miami, Tampa, Orlando, and Jacksonville each offer distinct investment profiles from luxury coastal to high-volume suburban growth corridors. The state's $330,000 median masks substantial regional variation.

New construction activity exceeds 100,000 annual building permits. Institutional investors, international capital, and operational sophistication set competitive expectations. For experienced operators, Florida's combination of tax efficiency, appreciation, and refinance velocity creates the highest-leverage investment environment in the nation.

What Works in Florida Right Now

Tampa New Construction Takeout & DSCR

Tampa's construction boom (20,000+ annual permits) supports builder financing and takeout. Finance development from land through vertical construction into permanent DSCR refinance. Multi-phase projects enable portfolio scaling.

Miami Luxury Repositioning & Bridge

Waterfront and high-end residential properties in Miami undergo renovation cycles. Bridge financing accelerates acquisition and value-add. Exit into permanent financing or stabilized rental income. Average project timelines are 8–12 months.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Florida have state income tax?
No. Florida has no state income tax, making it highly attractive for real estate investors. All capital gains, rental income, and business profits are tax-free at the state level, maximizing investment returns.
What are Florida's eviction laws?
Florida is landlord-friendly. Evictions require 3 days' notice and typically conclude within 2–3 weeks. No just-cause requirement allows lease termination for any reason. 'Pay or quit' notices are enforceable and routine.
Are there flood insurance or hurricane considerations?
Coastal and FEMA-mapped flood zones require flood insurance, which can cost $800–$2,000+ annually. Hurricane season (June–November) creates operational and insurance cost considerations. Inland markets offer lower insurance but face summer weather intensity.
What's the typical hold period for fix-and-flip projects in Florida?
5–8 months is standard for residential projects. Material and labor availability are excellent. Permitting is fast in most jurisdictions (2–4 weeks). Bridge financing with 9–12 month terms accommodates seasonal market conditions.
